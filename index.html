Sapi

Indonesian/Malay word for "Cow"

Definition

Sapi is the Indonesian and Malay word for cow or cattle. These domesticated animals are essential to agriculture and culture throughout Southeast Asia.

Cultural Significance

Cows hold great importance in Indonesian and Malaysian societies, serving as livestock for dairy, meat, and labor. In rural areas, they are often used for plowing fields and transportation.

Interesting Facts

  • Sapi are sacred in Hindu Balinese culture
  • Indonesia has several native cattle breeds including Sapi Bali
  • Beef (daging sapi) is a key ingredient in many Indonesian dishes
  • Traditional bull racing (karapan sapi) is popular in Madura
  • Cows provide milk (susu sapi) for dairy products

Common Phrases

  • Susu sapi - Cow's milk
  • Daging sapi - Beef
  • Sapi perah - Dairy cow
  • Ternak sapi - Cattle farming
